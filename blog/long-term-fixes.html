Long Term Fixes

[link—standalone]

Most of what I write and think about has to do with creating long-term plans. I'm not trying to rule the world or anything, just make it a better place. This does present problems, though, and while I try and order most of my choices toward the large ideological change I'd like to see in the world, that doesn't work really well in most cases. I've become aware of the drawbacks recently.

My attitude of "let's solve this problem permanently" sometimes gets in the way of solving a problem for now. I am, in many cases, letting perfect be the enemy of good, a thing I try to be really attuned to.

Instead of trying to forge a new path, I should figure out when course-correcting is good enough, and stick to that. Short term fixes, which I don't like to focus on or consider, are sometimes a good step up the staircase to the long term fix.

by Adam Thiede
