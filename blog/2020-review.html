2020 Review

[link—standalone]

2020 was a year. Burdensome, but satisfying. Made a lot of improvements, spent a lot of money on home office gear (as did everyone), cancelled a lot of online services, streamlined my personal computer setups, did some Advent of Code (until it got too hard), started a new job about 3 hours before lockdown, did a lot of RPM packaging, started soldering more stuff, started understanding myself better, getting along better with my family, learning to take control of things I should, and let go where I can't.

2 things to focus on, perpeually: Continuous Process Improvement, and Thankfulness/counting my blessings. Despite everything, life's still pretty good.
