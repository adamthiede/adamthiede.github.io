Turning The Cheek and Tech Independence

[link—standalone]

In Matthew 5, Jesus commands us not to take retaliatory action against evildoers.

Engaging in violence with our enemies would not only continue to allow them to damage their own souls, but would bring us down as well. If we are commanded to be perfect, this is not perfect action. Going the extra mile (even literally, in the case of one of Jesus' examples!) for those who bear us ill will is an act of service. Kindness and love inspire others to kindness and love, as hate and violence only perpetuate the same.

There is no better way to defuse an aggressive situation than by de-escalation. That is what Jesus is asking of us. He is not asking us to encourage or fan the flames of evil behavior. It was hard for me to pick this up at first. Why would the Lord ask us to encourage violence and abuse? He is not - love is willing the good of the other, and loving one's enemies means enabling good behavior, or defusing bad behavior. Like the Bishop in Les Misérables who told the police he had given to Jean Valjean the items that Valjean had stolen, our enemy can commit no crime if we freely give. This act of love shocked Jean Valjean, and inspired him to truly become a better man. Critically, these acts of selfless love do not take part in the sin of our enemies. They do not enable. They do not make us co-conspirators, leading the other to continue to sin. Love brings others out of sin and into freedom.

On Technology: when a large corporation abuses its users, the employees who take part in this are complicit. I worked at Oracle and helped build a product that spied on people. This was wrong. Those who use the products put forth by these companies are enabling their evil and should cease doing so. An act of love, in this case, is spending one's own money and time to help others break out of dependence on these companies. (Self-hosting email or XMPP or Nextcloud comes to mind.) De-escalation, for this situation, is just a peaceful refusal to use any of this bad technology. It is important to meet people where they are, and offer an olive branch, a compromise, with the understanding that the eventual goal is to disable all this abusive technology entirely by drying up its pool of users.

Standing against evil should not be about raising the sword and harming our enemies. Evil exists and attempts to corrupt each one of us, and the best way to stand against it is to love others into goodness through sacrifice and gentleness. This is a way I know how to help others. I know I am pushy or passionate at times, and could stand to improve. When I fail, dear reader, help me get back on the right track. I'll do what I can to help you, too.

by Adam Thiede
