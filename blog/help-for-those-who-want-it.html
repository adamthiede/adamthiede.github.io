Help For Those Who Want It

[link—standalone]

For much of my life I've been unofficially doing "technology consulting" for my friends, family, classmates, neighbors, and really anyone who asks. Anything from ripping CDs and DVDs and converting home movies, or wiring a sound system, to building PCs, providing purchasing advice, fixing software problems, and setting up a network-wide ad blocker in someone's home. All of this I have done for free, for people I know. I don't go out seeking people who need help or advertising myself, but I volunteer and make myself available for those in my circle.

Over time, I've learned that there are two types of assistance I can provide:

  1. Fixing problems with people's goods
  2. Filling gaps in people's knowledge

What I really want is to help people interested in improving their own knowledge. I want to make people smarter, not make people's stuff less broken until they break it again, because they've learned nothing. I would rather be a teacher, than a mechanic. Of course, sometimes it's a bit of both, but I prefer Number 2 - it's more empowering for the individual!

I will, as before, continue doing this stuff for free in my spare time, because I belive it makes the world a better place, I enjoy it, and I already have a full-time job that pays well enough. But I'm trying to make it more of a formal experience - I will write up an outline and a curriculum with what I'm willing and able to teach and start putting it into practice when I am asked to help.

With all that out of the way, here are some basic questions I propose, as a kind of advertisement.

  • Are you happy with your computers? (Phones, printers, Wi-Fi routers, those count too)
  • Do they do everything you want them to, and nothing you don't?
  • Are you concerned about the behavior of the companies that control your digital experience?
  • Do you think digital privacy, safety, and security are important?
  • Are you happy with how you interact with the Internet, and how it interacts with you?
  • Do you feel like you have to spend too much money on computers?
  • Are you a bit creeped out by computers sometimes?
  • Do you have questions about computers, but don't know who to trust, and where to start?

I can answer your quetions and solve your problems. I will help you with purchasing decisions, help you get more life out of your existing equipment, help you gain back some privacy and security, and in general make your digital life easier to understand, safer, and more free. There's a spectrum to everything, and we can take it as far as you want, depending on how much time and interest you have. Email me. Let's chat. I'd love to hear from you. I want to help you help yourself.
