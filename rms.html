If the recent RMS events have taught me anything, it's that you're not allowed to be neuroatypical on the Internet anymore. Those of us who can't quite interact "normally" (an unintentionally hurtful term) with other people will crawl back into our holes, because there's no place for us anymore. My kind isn't wanted, because we don't fit the perfect mold of what "normal" people behave like.

I do not want to come across as a defender of Richard Stallman. True, his accomplishments are legendary, but his personal behavior leaves much to be desired. RMS is very socially liberal, and I'm much closer to "traditional Judeo-Christian values" than any non-software-related opinions he holds. I wouldn't defend his positions, but his right and ability to express his positions is what got us the Free Software movement, period. His incessant annoying single-minded focus and inability to compromise his values or behavior is the reason the FSF exists. He changed the world for the better, and he did it because of his abnormality.

To thine own self be true. It seems that those who signed the RMS hate letter really just want to say, "Let's remove RMS from public positions, because he's a gross old creep that makes me feel uncomfortable, and isn't a good public representative of Free Software". I'm okay with that. He is kind of a gross old creep. But, "abuse", "transphobia", and "harrassment"? These, and all the even-worse accusations, are all completely provably false. Seriously. There's not a shred of evidence to substantiate any claim beyond one or two people saying "he made me a little uncomfortable several decades ago".

Like any adult, RMS apologizes for mistakes, and he changes his opinions. He's a human, and he's not the best, but he's not the worst either. Frankly he's kinda boring and irrelevant these days. So let's move on, but let's also dispense with the public lynching. It produces a chilling effect for all the people who want to try things, who are a little wacky and out there. It makes them scared to invent, to change the world.
Most things end not with a bang, bug with a whimper. And telling neuroatypical people that their not-cookie-cutter selves are not wanted in Free Software is how you remove all the interesting folks and whimper out this critically important movement in the computer space. It's how you destroy Free Software. And I don't think any of us want that.
