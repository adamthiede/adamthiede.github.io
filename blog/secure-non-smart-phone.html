Secure Non Smart Phone

[link—standalone]

In modern times, people communicate with each other via smartphones, mostly. Whether it's WhatsApp, iMessage, Matrix, email, Signal, or even SMS and phone calls, most people use a smartphone. That means I have to use a smartphone to receive communications from these smartphone-owners, as well. On smartphones we have the ability to choose secure communication. However, I do not want a smartphone, and all the baggage that comes with a full smartphone operating system. But opting out of the smartphone would also mean opting into communication surveillance (and lower quality communication overall), and I do not want that, either.

There are non-smart-phones ("dumb" phones, as they're called now) that are intelligently designed and fairly non-creepy, like the Light Phone and Punkt MP02, but those for some reason don't put security - or, if your family/friends only use some smartphone app, communication at all - on their list. Also, these devices with their basic SMS/phone call only communication are bizarrely expensive.

Smartphones are bad. That's something we can all agree on here. But the need for smartphones these days cannot be understated. Personally, I feel trapped in the walled garden full of parking apps, 2-factor authenticator apps, communication apps, apps necessary for work - and I'd guess most other people do too.

I propose a tool that is a communication device that puts security and privacy first, but is also not a smartphone. The communicator should look and act like a smartphone, but have only a small set of pared-down first-party programs that facilitate communication. The closest comparison would be an iPhone with only four core functions:

  1. Messaging. This encompasses voice calls, video calls, and text-based messages. It will integrate with Signal, Tox, Matrix, or whatever is deemed the best most friendly but also secure way to communicate. It should have robust APIs and support integrations from external services, so you can get non-human alerts. This is helpful for people in my line of work.
  2. Notes. People frequently have need to jot down a quick note. This program will store plain-text files in a folder and will be visible over USB. Encryption would be considered here.
  3. Settings. Of course you need a way to set up accounts, connect to networks, change accessibility preferences, set up a mobile hotspot, view data usage, and manage the darn thing. Updates for core software will be handled from here as well.
  4. Maps. One of the more common smartphone features people rely upon and would miss is a maps program. The maps program would allow offline navigation, encouraging local storage of map areas, and possibly integrate with ride-hailing services.
Functions not included in this device would be things like a music player, camera, web browser, video player, app store, and other features best left to other, larger devices.

Speaking of physical characteristics, here are the main goals for the communicator's physical shape:

  • Small size. Most phones these days are ridiculously huge. This should be iPhone 4S size, about. Since touch screens are less expensive than buttons (and allow for greater modularity) this would not have a physical tactile keyboard.
  • Removable, replaceable parts. The communicator's battery should be easily accessible. It should have an easily swappable SIM card (or two), and optionally a microSD card slot for storage of larger maps, or mass storage accessible via MTP.
  • Headphone jack with TRRS support. It's nice to walk around hands-free with a headset while taking a call, instead of performing neck-gymnastics or constantly switching hands. The communicator should be able to take advantage of modern headphones with in-line mics.
  • Standard connectivity. It should use modern WiFi and cellular radios, replaceable as new tech comes out. As many parts as possible should be pogo-pin connected to the mainboard, to facilitate easy removal. The charger should be USB-C or micro-USB, and that should be a choice users can make to suit their needs. The battery should be something that already exists and can be easily sourced, like an existing smartphone battery, or a BL-5C battery.
All in all, I'm not a manufacturer or a materials scientist. A device like this would be fantastic, and would be my ideal handheld communicator. Minimal required functionality, a true off switch, inexpensive upgrades or part replacements, and obviously 100% open source hardware and software.

This is an opinionated device, with very little functionality, made specifically for a small subset of people who have a complicated love/hate relationship with technology like mine. I don't expect it ever to happen, but it's nice to put into words. A man can dream, right?

Yes, I'm aware of the PinePhone. It gets pretty close, but it's enormous and more functional than I'd like. The device I'm proposing is much stricter and more opinionated than that.
