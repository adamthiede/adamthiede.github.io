COVID, Power, and Taking a Stand

Please don't, but you can skip to the good part if you're impatient, or already believe and trust me.

I love rules.

I love understanding rules, and following them. After all, living in a civilized society, we all agree to abide by a set of rules, and that's what keeps society civil, sane, and safe. Growing up in Oregon, aerial fireworks were theoretically illegal, but every July 4th, the neighborhood would agree to send some families across the river into Washington, to obtain the "illegals". (Yes, that's what we called them.) I was always distraught about this and eventually stopped participating in the fireworks mess altogether as a pre-teen, as a protest against this flagrant rule-breaking.

Rules are what keep our society safe. But they only work when the rules are reasonable and useful. Sometimes, someone refuses to leave a diner or the front of a bus, or uses the wrong drinking fountain on purpose, and breaks the rules. We agree that these were bad rules, stop enforcing them, and eventually take them out of the rule set. Critically, it must happen in this order. The rules are broken first, and then they are removed second. It takes a spark of inspiration before we all slowly, eventually come to the right conclusions. Often those in power seek to retain their power through the enforcement of dangerous or harmful rules. (See Gerrymandering, if you ever had any doubt) So it does us good to question rules, try and see the reasons behind them, and figure out if they're good for us in the long-term, or if they're mainly built to keep people in power, and trick the majority of the population into doing something harmful.

On Resisting For The Wrong Reasons

A lot of the more tech-savvy individuals these days seem to be a little hesitant on developing Artificial Intelligence, how far we should take it, and how fast we should get there. Hesitancy in the roll-out of a potentially world-altering technology is a good thing. Being cautious, looking before you leap, and trying to be prepared before diving headfirst into the unknowable future is a good thing.

Now that doesn't mean we should fight every rule, vote down every tax increase, throw eggs at politicians' cars, and drive on the wrong side of the road because the people in power are all bad. That's not the case. But we should be hesitant to be on their "side", because they are not on ours. We should be hesitant of accepting what they tell us without verification from those we do trust. Note that this mostly goes for people in rather powerful positions; the governor of your rural town is probably not mad with power. Take what I say with a grain of salt too - I'm rather imperfect myself.

So don't think of this piece as a resistance against authority because "F the police" or "Orange Man Bad" or "Let's Go Brandon" reasons. That's not it. If my government mandated spinach consumption, I wouldn't stop eating healthy just to spite them. It's just an application of healthy skepticism against what is being force-fed through every avenue these days. Seems like people used to encourage that a lot more often.

Here's The Good Part

I don't think the currently active coronavirus was developed on purpose and released on purpose. That's a little too far-fetched for me. It likely developed due to inhumane living conditions and a lack of cleanliness as most of us are aware. They've got it rough over there in China. But many politicians are known to espouse the "never let a good crisis go to waste" motto, and so this one's obviously being milked for all it's worth. Just like war, there are always groups that can stand to make a whole heckload of profit when things like this happen. Making folks afraid, making them distrust each other, and giving them an easy thing to buy or support to help quell that fear, is the oldest marketing trick in the book. Think of the children. You wouldn't want someone to break into your house, buy this alarm system/gun. The terrorists are coming, vote for me to bring the fight to them. You're being tracked, buy my VPN. (This is a good time to tell you I earn no money off of you visiting this site. I have no ads, no tracking, collect no information of any kind, and nobody pays me to write this. I hardly check logs on the webserver. It is costing me money to make this site available.)

So why isn't that classical healthy dose of skepticism being applied to the solutions that the government has proposed for COVID-19? Do people really just trust the US Government and Big Pharma now? Do we not remember the infinite war on terror, the constant support/ousting of dictators in other countries, and oh yeah, the NSA and Edward Snowden? Three years ago you could hardly hear someone say "Big Pharma" without spitting! They profit from our misery, keep us dependent on them with infinite booster shots and stimulus checks, keep us scared by locking us down and forcing us to wear masks, and keep us hating our neighbors through division and strife.

Think about the last two years. How have you been? Have you lost your job, lost loved ones to mental illness and suicide, dipped into your savings, tightened your belt, and become increasingly frazzled and short-tempered staying packed in a house with your whole family? Have things been pretty rough for you? I know they have been for me. Now think about the people running our governments, pharmaceutical companies, and technology corporations.

The people that hold the power have not had it any worse the last two years. They've only got more of what they crave. The only thing Apple and Google can agree on is a common method by which they can track users wherever they go! It's been 10 years with no Facetime on Android, but in less than six months both Apple and Google rolled out an interoperable contact tracing framework and built it into every phone. The government increases taxes, so that they can give some of the money back to you in the form of stimulus checks. They enjoy a dependent class. We're now saying the names of Big Pharma companies on a regular basis, and they're rolling in their blank checks from the governments. The money comes from your pocket.

Society seems to no longer be about how we can all agree to live alongside each other in harmony. It's about who can follow the ever-changing set of senseless rules and agree that we've always been at war with East Asia. (1984 reference about the government constantly changing history, for the unaware).

The Hill

I left my last job because they demanded evidence of a COVID-19 vaccine in order to continue working there. I know many people, regardless of their COVID-19 vaccination status, who agree on this point - the mandate is a violent power grab and it is our moral duty to resist this tyranny. If a employer wants to choose that, they are free to do so, but governments forcing employers, by way of OSHA or other federal standards, is wrong.

I can't tell you whether the vaccines are right for you or whether you should refuse them. But the mandate is wrong. Forcing people out of a job is wrong. If you're going to choose a hill to die on, this is it. This is a tipping point. The power grab has gone on long enough. Real folks have had a very hard time these last few years. Now we're creating a new class of untouchables out of the skeptics, just because some of them want to wait a little longer, like cautious A.I. researchers, or are a little skeptical, like an atheist in the deep South. It is wrong to cut off a large portion of society because of a legitimate decision or desire to move a little slower than warp speed.

Being an early adopter sucks - I know that from tech. It might be the greatest breakthrough since chemotherapy, but I'm not going to be an early adopter of a new type of medicine. You all have fun with the beta testing - I'm going to wait for the 1.0 immunization, or even better, a treatment for the illness, instead of an experimental, barely-tested treatment, with know knowledge of long-term effects.

And, speaking of tech, vaccine passports? Contact tracing? These are a disgusting overreach, a massive violation of individual (and group) privacy, and should have never been implemented. Not only do we already kind of force people to carry Apple or Google spyware device around with them everywhere they go, we're going to use that to track their location and who they're around in real time, and only allow them into places if they have one of these expensive medallions, proving they obeyed all the inane mandates. Whether or not you have received a COVID-19 shot, these are also incredibly evil mechanisms by which those in power seek to control and corral us, and you should avoid them.

Addressing those who are getting the vaccine, not because they want it, but just because they want things to go back to normal - the "go along to get along" crew:
This is a remarkably short-sighted and dull way of thinking. If you need a job, and they say "get the shot", and you get it despite not wanting to, you prove to yourself, your employer, and your family that you're a reed in the wind - you're weak. You prove that not only is your labor for sale, but your values are for sale too, and ultimately, your employer can make you do whatever they want, because you don't have a line you're not willing to cross. (The final line, of course, being "bodily autonomy", which you just gave up.) If you're willing to take experimental drugs to keep a job where they don't care about you, it's proof you're willing to do pretty much anything for money. It's cowardly, and it's greedy. Either you don't care that much about your personal values, or you care too much about money. Jobs will come and go. You can find work. There's a lot of work these days!

An exception can be made for those in positions of power who compromise themselves, so they can continue to use their power to protect others. You have to decide what's right for you. But a $12/hr job is not worth compromising your morals. You can find another one in a week.

If employers are willing to send their best and brightest packing because they refused some drugs on the grounds of some shaky government mandate, then what other stupid reasons are they going to find to send you packing someday? They don't even need a reason. Maybe you're out the door for refusing to vote for Nader next election, or for refusing to buy stock in the CFO's cousin's sister-in-law's cryptocurrency startup.

The Consequences

I am sick and tired of reasonably intelligent adults acting like the best thing we can do is go along with this nonsense and hope that at some point big tech will stop spying on us, the government will give up its power, big pharma will lower their budgets and be content with shipping less medicine, and new rules and mandates will keep us safe. Getting everyone vaccinated will never get us out of the pandemic. Heck, they've been honest and upfront since almost the beginning - if you get the shot you still have to keep wearing a mask! Giving them what they want will not make things better for us. We should all be familiar with slippery slopes and escalation. (Some people call them a logical fallacy, but that's usually because the examples are comically bad, not because they don't happen in the real world - they do. Appealing to authority is a logical fallacy too, by the way.)

It's never been about safety. It's never been about ending the virus. (People with the vaccines can get COVID-19!) It's about how much freedom they can take from us and how much power they can amass without open revolt. I don't care which geriatric old man you voted for; this isn't about that. We all need our lives back. If we can't resist being forced to take drugs, then what is left that we can we even say no to? If not now then when?

They are not done making you do stuff. Maybe not this time, but next time, it'll be something you don't like. But by then it'll be too late. The precedents have been set. An adversarial government is winning. Big Pharma is winning. Big Tech is winning. They're doping you up, mentally and physically, making you complacent and compliant. We do not need violent revolt or riots in the streets. What we need is just a healthy dose of skepticism and disbelief of those in power. They do not have your best interests at heart. They have profit and power in mind, and by following bad rules, we're giving it to them.

I know it's hard. I like rules too. But we have to take a stand. No more lockdowns, no more mandates, no more spying and fear and pitting us against one another. Hopefully it's not too late.

Postscript

If you're that afraid of COVID-19 and other illnesses, wear a mask yourself, or stay at home. I'm not, so I shouldn't be forced to.

There are only six US states that mandate masks, and many states have blocked vaccine passports, with only a few really looking into implementing them. They're the obvious states on the West coast. So if things aren't looking too bad for you yet, remember that this stuff bleeds inward from the coasts. It never goes the other way around. So keep an eye out and resist where appropriate.
