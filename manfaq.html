DESCRIPTION
There's a lot that goes on in my day, but work takes up the biggest slice. My job goes by many titles - Site Reliability Engineer, Systems Administrator, DevOps Engineer, Systems Analyst, IT Guy, "Computer Guy" (my personal favorite). I know loads about computers, in both a specific and general sense, and I am paid to make them work well. I can do anything from setting up a laptop with a printer all the way up to configuring network infrastructure or managing systems and applications at scale. You should care because I can help you do anything you want with computers!

This site is both a portfolio and blog. The blog is focused on the values of freedom, independence, and longevity, and how computers help or hurt those goals. Computer Freedom is one of the most important issues of our time - since computers are everything and everywhere, we must ensure the computers we use respect individual freedom and privacy. I value simplicity, interoperability, stability, security, and reproducibility, and only Free Software will give you that - anything else is a mystery.

FREQUENTLY ANSWERED QUESTIONS
Q: Can you fix my computer?
A: Yep. Just give me a call or an email and tell me when you want to bring it to my house for me to work on.

Q: How do I get digital privacy/security/sovereignty without putting in effort?
A: No.

Q: What computer stuff should I use?
A: I have a recommended list of software here. Buy used hardware that's well supported by Linux and OpenBSD. Usually used ThinkPads or Dell Latitudes or HP EliteBooks. Aim for 3-5 years old.
