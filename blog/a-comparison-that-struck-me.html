A Comparison That Struck Me

[link—standalone]

A thought struck me (ow) today preparing for guests.

The way our state and federal governments in the US have been handling the COVID-19 pandemic is akin to an abusive partner (or spouse, parent, what have you). Here's how.

  • Telling you to stay home forever
  • Preventing you from going to a job, cutting off your financial independence
  • Making you feel guilty for seeing friends and family ("superspreader event")
  • Becoming your primary source of income (stimulus packages) so that you're dependent on them, and only them
  • Putting spyware on your phone (contact tracing, thanks Apple & Google) to track who you come into contact with
  • Allowing you limited contact with others, but only via heavily monitored and restricted methods (zoom, MS Teams) and via devices they already monitor
  • Restricting travel
  • Forcing face coverings to hide emotional state
  • Constant gaslighting - "masks aren't safe", "masks will save you", "two weeks to flatten the curve", "you can go out", "you can't go out", "only one shot", "booster every year", "schools are open", "schools are closed"
  • Sock-puppeteering of authority figures you've never heard of to seem convincing

I could go on but I've got other stuff to do today. It just seems like no matter which way you turn, you're over a barrel. Nobody knows what to belive, everyone's scared, and we're all being manipulated by a government and megacorps that don't care about anything but lining their pockets, and increasing their control.

Even if we do what they say, get all the shots, wear masks forever, never see our friends - I don't see this getting any better. It's not about the disease. It's never been about the disease. It's about control. It's about how much they can get away with. "Never let a good crisis go to waste."

by Adam Thiede
