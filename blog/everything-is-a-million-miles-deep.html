Everything Is A Million Miles Deep

[link—standalone]

Everything is a million miles dep. There is no end to how far down the stack someone's preferences go, and people have opinions about every little thing.

Somewhere out there, in a thread in a forum in an obscure corner of the Internet, ther are a couple dozen real individuals passionately banging out paragraph after paragraph on the most unimportant attirbute of something you and I don't even think about. Could be the different types of glass used in old milk bottles. The shape of the rungs of a ladder of one model of Ikea bed no longer sold. The ink on the side of a specific model of car tire. Colors of tile grout.

Anything and everything is fair game. Because people so individual, so unique, there is no limit to the depth of their fascination with trivialities. One of my favorite examples is this LKML thread discussing terminal column width and code styling.

Many people's preferences stop at operating system - Linux, BSD, or macOS? After that, you can discuss BSD flavor/Linux distribution. Then desktop environment/window manager. People normally have preferences regarding GNOME/KDE. Even then, yet further down the stack, is your choice of terminal emulator - the default GNOME Terminal/Konsole, xterm, terminator, or alacritty? Next, vim or emacs?

This discussion goes down further than that. After all those choices have been made, they discuss the width to which they should expand their terminal. 80, 90, or 100 characters? And there's hundreds, if not thousands, of words being written on the subject right now, by dozens of individuals with opinions, passions, and experience with a thing most people have never even thought about.

Humans are fascinating.

by Adam Thiede
