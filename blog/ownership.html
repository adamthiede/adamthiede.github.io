Ownership

[link—standalone]

I've lived in an apartment for 2 months now, after living in an owned home the last few years. I now know I vastly prefer the latter, as well as running Linux, self-hosting, and not using a smartphone. Here's what I like about ownership.

When I own something, I am in control. For better or for worse, I have both the ownership and responsibility. If something breaks, I can and must fix it. When I rent something, I must wait for the real owner to fix it, and they often take longer and do a worse job than I would. At a job of mine when we hosted our own email server, it almost never went down, and if it did, it was quick to get back up or fail over, because of our high redundancy, low complexity, and complete ownership. O365, AWS, GCP, and others went down a few times, for multiple hours at a time, during that 2 year period. Our uptime was better because we had an owned system that was less complex.

If I want to change something, I can. Simple as that. Our cabinets here don't have handles and my work Macbook doesn't have a competent window manager. I would change both, if I could.

Things aren't changed out from under my feet when I own something. Ownership is stable and predictable. Rent will increase, user interfaces will change, APIs will go away, the owners might decide to shut the place down or turn off the service - I live at the whims of others. While I own the movies on my NAS and hard drive, but I've had streaming or digital "purchases" take my stuff away before. There are plenty of people running KDE3 and GNOME2 (Trinity, MATE) because they forked the old versions they liked and kept them around. Imagine if you could keep, customize, and update Windows 98 or iOS 6 yourself.

Ownership is harder. There's more stuff you have to deal with that most would rather not. But I like stability, privacy, security, and living on my terms, not the terms of some arbitrary master who may not share my values. Unless you own your stuff, use open protocols, and make sure you are in control of as much as you can be, you will spend your whole life getting the rug pulled out from under you. That's frustrating!

Living in this awful apartment has whiplashed me so hard that I want to start hosting my own email.*

*For the non-technical: self-hosting email is not generally considered a good idea on a small scale or individual basis, because the potential risks are too high for something as critical to modern life as email. It is a siren's song for sysadmins, both green and seasoned alike, as controlling one's own email does offer a great deal of control over personal data.
