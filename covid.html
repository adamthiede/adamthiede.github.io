COVID, Power, and Taking a Stand

I love rules.

I love understanding rules, and following them. After all, living in a civilized society, we all agree to abide by a set of rules, and that's what keeps society civil, sane, and safe. Growing up in Oregon, aerial fireworks were theoretically illegal, but every July 4th, the neighborhood would agree to send some families across the river into Washington, to obtain the "illegals". (Yes, that's what we called them.) I was always distraught about this and eventually stopped participating in the fireworks mess altogether as a pre-teen, as a protest against this flagrant rule-breaking.

Rules are what keep our society safe. But they only work when the rules are reasonable and useful. Sometimes, someone refuses to leave a diner or the front of a bus, or uses the wrong drinking fountain on purpose, and breaks the rules. We agree that these were bad rules, stop enforcing them, and eventually take them out of the rule set. Critically, it must happen in this order. The rules are broken first, and then they are removed second. It takes a spark of inspiration before we all slowly, eventually come to the right conclusions. Often those in power seek to retain their power through the enforcement of dangerous or harmful rules. (See Gerrymandering, if you ever had any doubt) So it does us good to question rules, try and see the reasons behind them, and figure out if they're good for us in the long-term, or if they're mainly built to keep people in power, and trick the majority of the population into doing something harmful.

On Resisting For The Wrong Reasons

A lot of the more tech-savvy individuals these days seem to be a little hesitant on developing Artificial Intelligence, how far we should take it, and how fast we should get there. Hesitancy in the roll-out of a potentially world-altering technology is a good thing. Being cautious, looking before you leap, and trying to be prepared before diving headfirst into the unknowable future is a good thing.

Now that doesn't mean we should fight every rule, vote down every tax increase, throw eggs at politicians' cars, and drive on the wrong side of the road because the people in power are all bad.
