This past year I've been playing a lot of Dungeons & Dragons with some new friends. The drive is long, it's late in the evening, but I keep coming back. It's a little less about the game, and more about the fun I have with my party members. It's fun to play, and interacting with people with the front of an alternative personality is much less taxing than actual human interaction.
As much as I'll defend video games as a valid method of entertainment, they don't even come close to in-person tabletop games. It's true that interaction like that is exhausting, but socialization is very important. Often I've not wanted to go, but I'm letting other people down when I don't go, and it does end up being good for my mental health. Maybe it's helping me get better in a way.
