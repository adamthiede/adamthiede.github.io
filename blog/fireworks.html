Fireworks

[link—standalone]

Fireworks are not good. Annually, I am reminded of this fact, when my neighbors gather to steal hours of my sleep in exchange for making me think there's a firefight happening outside my window.

Zero days during the year am I allowed to walk down the street late at night, yelling and screaming, discharging a firearm into the ground and throwing rocks at my neighbors' windows. (Many fireworks this year sounded like a handful of pebbles being chucked at my window, or, if you've had the good fortune to have your house shot by a shotgun, that too.) Yet this is what fireworks are like. The only difference is that shooting the ground would be safer.

Around 23:15 last night, two hours past when I normally fall asleep, I opened my blinds to see how much longer this would continue. It's two kids lighting little fireworks that just scream and make no pretty lights. They're not placing them safely - they are holding them, lighting them, and throwing them into a cardboard box about a yard behind a car. I watch for about one minute and during this time I see a firework shoot out of the box, almost hit one of the kids, and pass through a small tree next to them before exploding about 20 feet off the ground. The next one escapes the cardboard box and explodes in my driveway. I watched them for one minute. I can't imagine what I didn't see.

Fireworks are impossible to be done safely. When those that employ fireworks are typically pretty awful at driving and using computers and exercise, they can't be trusted to perform a dangerous task safely that typically requires a professional to get right.

Fireworks are done at night, in one of the driest parts of the year, when people are typically home, on a holiday. All those converge to tell me that a) the adults using fireworks are likely going to have a bit of alcohol in them, b) it's already not a safe time of year to do it, and c) the reduced visibility of the night makes it an even worse idea.

Any excuses people give for fireworks can also be used on drunk driving, and map perfectly.

Fireworks killed 5 and injured about 10,000 people in 2018. "All of these fatalities were associated with reloadable aerial devices" is a pretty damning sentence. Guess what's the favorite type of firework? Guess what's illegal in Oregon, where I grew up, but everyone would go across the border to the "Ill Eagle" firework stand in Washington to get? Reloadable aerials!

It's not possible for people to get this right. They're too dangerous in the hands of non-professionals, they go on far too late in the night, keeping up people who'd rather be sleeping, and they create a bunch of waste and trash in the streets. Any other day of the year it's frowned upon for me to mow my lawn at 22:00 (later would be worse), throw rocks at neighbors' windows, or fire my firearm into the street at 4AM. It's disruptive and prevents people from sleeping.

Most of all, the majority of people that launch fireworks just aren't thinking about it. If they were, they'd stop at a reasonable hour, have some safety measures in place, only do it in a remote location away from most houses, not launch aerials, and do it during the daytime when it's lightly drizzling out. The way it's done now is wrong, and while I can't tell everyone what to do, I know that this is dangerous, and anyone who takes a minute to think about it should come to the same conclusions.

by Adam Thiede
