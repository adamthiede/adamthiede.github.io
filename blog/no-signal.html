No Signal

[link—standalone]

I have deleted my Signal account, after years of it being my primary messaging system, and years of fervent encouragement of others to use it.

Signal was a simple, secure, open source messaging program. It's turned into a service with contact discovery, a cryptocurrency wallet, and now, centralized social media. That's not a thing that I want to use.

This is all in an effort to increase the reach, get more users, have more people using Signal instead of Telegram or WhatsApp or whatever. Sure, I get it. But I don't want to use Telegram or WhatsApp or anything that looks like it. I don't want apps. I don't want social media. I don't want a smartphone. I want a computer program that offers encrypted communication via a standard, decentralized, ubiquitous protocol. That's it.

If you'd like to reach me, send an XMPP message to my email address, or call my phone number, or send me an email.

A side benefit of this: I've lost the final reason to use a smartphone.

I'm free!

by Adam Thiede
