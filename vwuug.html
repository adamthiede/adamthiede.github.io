Unix User Group for Vancouver, Washington, USA


A Linux User Group exists in Portland, and they're Unix-Friendly, but I'd like this one to focus more generally Unix/BSD as well. Also, for folks in Vancouver, traveling into Portland is a hassle during the week.

Not only does "Unix and Unix-like" encompass Linux as well, but I belive the BSDs and modern Unixes are more technically interesting than Linux. Hopefully this can be a group of like-minded Unix users that can gather, discuss what we are up to, learn about tools, share ideas, and make a few friends.

I have no idea how to start one of these things, so any help is appreciated. Please email me and and we can get started organizing.

-What's it about?

-Unix, Linux, Free/Libre/Open-Source Software. Could include hardware hacking as well. I don't want to limit it before the first meeting!


-How much structure?

-Sort of structured. If we have several meetings in a row with no speakrs, fine. We can just shoot the breeze and install a random Linux distro in a VM, or have an ad-hoc discussion.


-Projects, challenges, etc?

-Sure! Sounds great especially if we don't anticipate speakers or fixed subjects. Not homework!


-Learning or just chit-chat or pure fun?

-If we do have a speaker, it would be nice to have them teach us something, but maybe they just tell us about their experiences.

-Hobbyist group - no shilling work services, unless it's asked for.


-Where would we host it?

-Coffee shop? High School? WSU Vancouver? The library? Current stay-at-home conditions mean we could use Jitsi as well (and for every in person meeting too, might as well broadcast it).
