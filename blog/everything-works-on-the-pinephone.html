Everything Works on the Pinephone

[link—standalone]

What happened?

As of today, I got phone calls, data, SMS, MMS, and reliable Internet communication working on the Pinephone. That means everything that one would reasonably expect a smartphone to do, this thing does it. (If you don't know what the Pinephone is, it's a Desktop Linux computer in the size and shape of a smartphone.) I am attempting to switch to using a Linux computer as my personal smartphone.

Why should you care?

I've explained my dissatisfaction with Signal before so I won't rehash it, but as of today, Signal is now a low-priority communication platform for me. I will not be immediately responsive there as I have been in the past.

How can you get in touch with me?

If you want to get ahold of me:

  1. Chat with XMPP - address is just my email address
  2. Email me
  3. Call me
  4. Matrix (email me if you are curious)
Do not send me SMS or MMS messages. Really. Don't. Email me instead.

Why?

I don't like smartphones. This "smartphone" device respects me. It lets me do what I want with it. It's as simple, or complicated, as I want it to be.
