If you want to give me a gift, I thank you, but please reconsider. I am bad at receiving and do not want gifts. But if you must, please do so according to the following guidelines. Items are listed in order of preference. I am quite particular about stuff ownership and what goods I let in my home.


  • I like books, especially technical books. I prefer physical books, but DRM-free ePub files are also a lovely gift. My Amazon wish list has some great suggetions.
  • An LWN Gift Certificate, a way for me to read subscriber-only articles on a high quality Linux news publication.
  • One can never own too many kettlebells. (national shop) These are good gifts for me.
  • Bandcamp is the place I buy music. They offer gift cards that can be exchanged for DRM-free, lossless audio files.
  • Coffee beans, tea, and high-quality craft alcoholic beverages are also wonderful gifts.
  • I have enough clothing, am particular about how clothing fits, and my body is not the size or shape you think it is. I will deal with this myself. Steph and Tom, you are the exceptions to this rule.
  • I like computers and "computer stuff", but am very particular; please don't get me a computer or anything with a computer in it.
  • A notable exception is Thinkpads, or other systems at least 10 years old - if you have an old computer, I will be overjoyed to play with it. It doesn't even have to be clean - I enjoy cleaning machines and can do it without damaging them.
  • These are the places I accept money. If you direct me how to spend it (in abstract) I will abide by the suggestion, i.e. "Put it in your child's 529" or "Buy your wife some art supplies".
  • Lastly, please do not feel pressured to get me gifts on holidays or birthdays. I am personally terrible at gift-giving and will probably not reciprocate. Let's instead have a meaningful conversation on my back porch over a beverage - I guarantee the memory of that will last longer than whatever you give me.
