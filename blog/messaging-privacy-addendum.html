messaging privacy addendum

[link—standalone] Update to this thing because I have hooked another Windows/iPhone user on the Free Software drug/religion/cult.
Chrome has a built-in malware scanner that scans your hard drive for malware that targets Chrome, and not much else. Chrome is proprietary software whose code you can't see. Chrome makes a ton of encrypted network requests that you can't see. Chrome is verifiably scanning and reading files on your hard drive and making lots of network requests.
Windows 10 has a keylogger [link 1] [link 2] that is turned on by default. Windows tracks and reports your activity. Windows makes a ton of network requests you can't see. Windows includes a keylogger.
Are you getting it?
You can turn these things off, apparently. There's a button, a switch, a checkbox, whatever. But the software doesn't stop phoning home. The behavior maybe stops. You have no idea. Here's a not-plausible-but-you-can't-prove-me-wrong scenairo - you click the "turn off keylogging" switch, and your computer immediately pings a Microsoft response team, who TURNS UP the keylogging, or highlights your profile in red, or pays closer attention to you, since you care more about privacy. You turn off the iCloud backups on your iPhone, but instead of that, they just exfiltrate tiny packets that they say are "diagnostic and usage data" or whatever, and those are compressed, encrypted packets of keystrokes and sound files from the microphone.
If the operating systems, programs, hardware, and service you use are not open-source, freedom-respecting, privcy-respecting tools, then THEY DO NOT CARE ABOUT YOU. They will do whatever they can to make a buck or to stay in business, whether that means selling your info to advertisers, cooperating with government agencies, or pulling all your data on to their servers just because.
And the best part is - I can provide a hundred examples of how proprietary software abuses you. You can provide NONE where open-source software abuses the rights of the user.
If you're going to live in a techno-centric society (you need smartphones to pay for parking - face it, we're cyborgs at this point) you need to understand how to live in here without getting your rights abused every day by these mega-corps that just want to squeeze every bit of data and money out of you that they can.
by Adam Thiede
