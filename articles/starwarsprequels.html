Improving the Plot of the Star Wars Prequels

The Star Wars prequels have interesting stories inside them, begging to get out. Unfortunately the released films are not very good - the writing and acting don't make a great pair sometimes, characters like Dooku are completely wasted, and and Anakin's story is not as compelling or well-thought-out as it could be. Before church today, my wife and I got to talking about how these problems could be solved.

And yes, the prequels have some interesting political plot points, but if we ultimately want this to be a personal narrative about Anakin and Luke, those should play a much smaller role, and we should spend far more time on the individual's character development.

Episode 1

In Episode 1, Anakin's story could have been more compelling by having him be a little older, more mature, or more jaded. He lives as a slave in a world where only power and money are respected. Podraces are organized by the mafia (Hutts) for entertainment - it's little better than a gladiatorial arena, since we're told ow dangerous it is. Anakin's sense of morals would have to be a bit off-kilter because of that, too. A slave dreaming of emancipation twisted into a lust for power seems like a great way to set up his character. He podraces not because "it's fun" but instead because it's a way to feel alive, powerful, or free; or some delusion that he can get out of slavery by podracing.

Anakin leaving his mother should be a much harder and more painful for the both of them. His eventual goal is to join the Jedi, gain power, wait until he's finally out of his ten years of strict monastic training, and then finally he should be free to come back and free his mother from slavery. Qui-Gon deliberately wants to separate Anakin from his mother and train him in isolation, because that's how Jedi do things, but in Episode 1 Anakin just seemed happy to be going on an adventure. I know it is difficult to get that kind of acting out of a child, but that's why I suggest he should be a bit older. The council's argument of "he's too old to be trained" would have a little more weight if Anakin was about 12. With the age gap between Anakin and Padme a little smaller, there is an actual potential for some innocent romance, too. 9-year-old Anakin isn't really old enough to be thinking romantically about anyone. In addition, it would be more compelling if Qui-Gon has a history of picking up talented youngsters from unlikely places. Obi-Wan could be slightly younger, or mention that he has a similar origin story to Anakin, and that could help make them good friends straight away. After Qui-Gon's death, the council could decide to keep the two boys together, since they're so close already and had endured some trauma with the loss of their master.

Speaking of this - Qui-Gon's death should have had a visible reaction from Anakin. There should have been a scene in which he found out about the death Qui-Gon and felt sad or betrayed. Qui-Gon was quite possibly the first good man and father figure in Anakin's life - he fought for him, freed him, and pushed his superiors in to allow his training and adoption to a better life where his skills would be valued. Maybe there should be a scene or two where Anakin steals or bullies someone else because that's all he knows, and Qui-Gon has a teaching moment about morals with him. This loss should feel far more personal than it did - like everything in the prequels, they were always in a rush, and it would feel better if more time was spent on Tatooine for relationships to form. We're making "Anakin feels powerless over the loss of a loved one" a theme here!

Episode 2

The only good thing about this movie is the Obi-Wan Detective B-plot. Anakin and Padme's romance could have been handled a lot better. Again, the timeline seemed compressed - it felt like they were only in hiding a few weeks maybe? I think it should have been months, or even a year. It's good that already Anakin starts off nervous but enthusiastic to see Padme again, but that should fade away upon realizing he was going to separated from Obi-Wan for a while. Obi-Wan is his mentor, religious brother, and basically his only friend. They've endured a lot together, and although we can't see that because of the time skip between movies, leaning on it with conversation and acting would be good.

Obi-Wan should accept the council's decision to send them off on separate missions, since being a senator's body guard isn't a complicated task. Anakin should be frustrated at Obi-Wan's serene acceptance of the council's will - he should hope that Obi-Wan would fight for them to stay together, and express that in anger to his brother. Remember, in this version, Anakin's a bit older and very innately powerful, so maybe he and Obi-Wan don't have a teacher/student relationship, more like partners, but Obi-Wan just has a bit of seniority. Rash and cavalier Anakin makes a good foil to measured and patient Obi-Wan.

After their separation, Anakin should be brooding that he's got a simple job just being a bodyguard, and Obi-Wan gets a cool assignment - remember Anakin's always looking for ways to test his power and and take risks to get stronger. Suddenly the assassination plot comes up, and now he's excited to be defending Padme. Anakin should fend off or defeat several assassins or attackers, and Padme should be fooled by his seeming selflessness for her, and fall for him a bit. The rest of the movie can progress as it did, but maybe take a bit longer on both of their assignments. Anakin deciding to go to Tatooine because he has a vision and fears his mother's death is great, and his anger at his own powerlessness is a well-written scene. His confession to Padme about slaughtering the town of tusken raiders should shock her into distancing herself from him, instead of marrying him. The two should have an "I know I shouldn't" on-again-off-again relationship in Episode 3.

In the fight with Dooku, Yoda should not show up - Yoda does not own a lightsaber. Dooku should easily best Obi-Wan, maybe suprirsing him with Lightning, and Anakin, who is better in a fight, should impress Dooku with his strength. Intrigued in this power he's never seen before, Anakin cautiously battles Dooku while inquiring about the power Dooku has and how he obtained it. Dooku can plant seeds of distrust in the Jedi here, and escape by promising to train Anakin in secret. Already distrusting the council after months/years of separation from Obi-Wan and what he considers inadequate training and trivial missions, Anakin could be intrigued, and the slow burn to the dark side could begin much earlier than it did.

Episode 3

In Episode 3, Anakin's thrown himself into his work. Back with Obi-Wan again, he's excited to be fighting a war in which he can fly fast ships, fight powerful enemies, and lead armies into battle. On leave he can spar with Dooku in secret. Wartime loss of life and Anakin's rapid increase in skill can grant him Jedi Master status and his own apprentice faster than Obi-Wan. Like in the real movie, this is mostly due to Palpatine's insistince that his favorite Jedi (and one of the most successful military leaders) be given a prominent position. Anakin hands down Palpatine's orders to a resentful council, features prominently in military propaganda, and as Palpatine's liason to the council, gets some government airtime and and makes warmongering speeches.

His attachment to Padme is tenuous at best at this point. It doesn't make sense that Vader would not search for his offspring, especially on his own home planet with his own family! So instead, maybe his death-vision is of Obi-Wan, or his Clone Wars TV show apprentice Ahsoka, both fast friends and brothers in arms. His dwindling relationship with Padme eventually results in her pregnancy, but she takes a sabattical and keeps it a secret from Anakin. Given her peace-loving nature, Padme is beginning to distrust him and doesn't want a warrior involved in childrearing.

The battle with Dooku should end differently. Anakin can battle Dooku, but deciding he's not through with his secret master, and that it's "not the Jedi way", denies Palpatine's "kill him" order. Palpatine decides to show off his power, and instead kills Dooku himself. He reveals to Anakin that he is a master of the force, and had trained Dooku. They don't even have to use the word "Sith" at this point, just that there's a "gray area" between the light and the dark, and that more power can be found there, if one is strong enough to control it. Anakin of course believes that he can, and trusting Palpatine, requests his tutelage. Palpatine in due time reveals he has the power to prevent people from dying, and Anakin is intrigued - the death of his mother, Qui-Gon, many comrades in the war, and visions of the death of Obi-Wan and/or Ahsoka have driven him to search for greater power, ostensibly to protect his friends.

Of course at some point Obi-Wan, who may not have been much of a fighter but is a great detective, figures out something's up about Anakin and Palpatine. Anakin confesses to his friend that Palpatine is a master of the force, and is teaching him to protect the people he cares about. Only through Palpatine can he have the power to prevent people from dying. Obi-Wan is grateful but still suspicious and wary. He cautions Anakin about the dark side, but Anakin misinterprets this as jealousy about Anakin's meteoric rise to power.

Anakin goes to Palpatine, and Obi-Wan goes to the council. Palpatine explains that he's had to hide his power all this time because he fears the council's strict rules about the Force, and obviously he's a good guy, just has a power that the Jedi are maybe uncomfortable with. Obi-Wan, having fought Darth Maul, is aware of the threat the Sith pose and is convinced Palpatine is a Sith Lord. The council agees, Yoda and Mace Windu having sensed similar things. With all three in agreement, Yoda sends Mace and Obi-Wan, along with Anakin's apprentice Ahsoka, go to confront Palpatine. Anakin defends Palpatine, and we can still use the "you turned her against me" line, but this time it's about Ahsoka instead of Padme. They battle, Mace is killed, Anakin is severely wounded, because Ahsoka and Obi-Wan both couldn't bring themselves to kill their friend. Palpatine is disfigured. Palpatine uses his mysterious power to escape with Anakin's body. Palpatine does not have a lightsaber either.

Order 66 ensues. Obi-Wan and the remaining Jedi go into hiding or get killed. Having known for many years about his relationship with Padme, Obi-Wan goes to visit her because he thinks she's in danger. She reveals that she has children by Anakin, and that she's kept it a secret. Obi-Wan is surprised - the secret was kept well. Anakin arrives, disfigured and half-transformed into the Vader suit. He is about to engage Obi-Wan, a fight that Obi-Wan would lose, but Ahsoka ambushes Anakin. Anakin kills her. Witness to all this, Padme fully realizes the monster Anakin's become and flees with Obi-Wan.

She reluctantly agrees to give up her son to Anakin's family, and goes into hiding herself on Aleraan with Leia. As she did so many years ago, she disguises herself as a maid and caretaker in the Organa household, but privately the family knows the deal.

After many battles with Jedi and many wounds, Anakin now is completely suited up as Darth Vader - more machine than man. He has fully succumbed to the dark side in his quest for power. Busy as he is as the military leader of the new Empire, he can't spend all his time searching for an old flame and a crafty Jedi that can't fight very well. His search ends, but the pain and anger remain.

Anyway, that's about it. I really enjoyed writing this! It was a fun creative exercise. I think the bones of a good story are there, but this just modifies a bit to make it a little less political and a little more personal. And of course, 50% of the credit goes to Stephanie.
