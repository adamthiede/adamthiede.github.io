Adam's "Wishlist"

If you want to give me a gift, I thank you, but please reconsider. I am bad at receiving and do not want gifts. But if you must, please do so according to the following guidelines. Items are listed in order of preference. I am quite particular about stuff ownership and what goods I let into my home.


  • I like books, especially technical books - Linux, BSD, systems administration, cyber security, programming languages, and the like. I prefer physical books, or DRM-free ePub or PDF files. O'Reilly and No Starch Press have lots of good books. Fiction or philosophy are also welcome.
  • An LWN Gift Certificate, a way for me to read subscriber-only articles on a high quality Linux news publication.
  • One can never own too many kettlebells. (national shop) These are good gifts for me.
  • Bandcamp is the place I buy music. They offer gift cards that can be exchanged for DRM-free, lossless audio files.
  • I have a microelectronics wishlist at adafruit. I write code that runs on these things and they do useful stuff in the real world. They're cheap and very useful. Basically you can't go wrong with anything from Adafruit or SparkFun.
  • Coffee beans, tea, and high-quality craft alcoholic beverages are also wonderful gifts.
  • Thinkpads, desktops, and other old computers, working or not, will occupy me for a while. Working or not, clean or not, this is a good free gift you can give me.
  • Instead of me, give something to my wife or my mother.

What Not To Do

  • Do not get me clothing or shoes. I have enough clothing, am particular about how clothing fits, and my body is not the size or shape you think it is. Steph and Tom, you are the exceptions to this rule. Socks are fine.
  • Don't get me a new computer, or anything with a computer-y bits (especially wireless communications) in it.
  • Lastly, please do not feel pressured to get me gifts on holidays or birthdays. I am personally terrible at gift-giving and will probably not reciprocate. Let's instead have a meaningful conversation on my back porch over a beverage - I guarantee the memory of that will last longer than whatever you give me.
