So you've decided "big tech" has no place in your life anymore. You've made your first step on the road to Digital Freedom and Technological Independece.

First of all, why would anyone want to do this? It seems hard, and computers are hard enough as it is already. Are there any benefits to reducing dependence on these companies?

Consider the following:

  • What would happen if your Google account was deleted? What would you lose access to?
  • And your Apple iCloud account? If someone gained access to that, what devices would they be able to compromise?
  • Do you trust Microsoft to keep you safe and not share your data with governments or advertisers?
  • Are you comfortable with Facebook knowing every website you visit? When there's a data breach, that data becomes public.

These scenarios are more common than you'd think. No one is out of bounds, and there's often no recourse - once your account is gone, or your data deleted, it's gone forever. A better question is why wouldn't anyone want to do this?

Assuming you don't trust Apple, Google, Amazon, and Microsoft with your data, and the keys to your digital life, I'll help you go about removing them from your life. Use the navigation at the top and pick a section.
