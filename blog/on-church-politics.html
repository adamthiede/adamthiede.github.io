On Church Politics

[link—standalone]

Occasionally I will find myself in a situation where I have to defend something stupid the Catholic Church has done. ("The Catholic Church" here refers to the organization and its human leadership, not doctrine.) This is not my job and I am not good at it. Usually it comes up with a family member or two who is really into Church Politics, or some friends who are non-practicing, non-denominational, or fallen-away Christians.

St. Peter, the man Jesus chose to lead his Church, denied the Lord three times on the day of his death; Jesus told him a few hours before he was going to do it, and Peter still did it! The Church is made of flawed humans, and they (we) make many mistakes, but that doesn't make the teachings any less true or God any less real. If we want to go all Cancel Culture on anyone who does anything wrong even once, and disregard all the good things he or she does, that leaves us in a pretty sorry state. Might as well throw all art and science out the window.

The impact most people will have on Church politics is vanishingly small. Nobody I know lives in Vatican City or Rome (Except for my cousin about 20 years ago). All we can do, as "normal" folks, Americans, half a world away from all this stuff, is to pray for the Church and its leaders, and try to find a good parish to go to, and support it. Be a friend to your Priest! We will make a greater impact at home, weathering the storms of life, and being good examples to those around us.

On the flip side, writing vicious articles about dead men who can't defend themselves or spreading around the latest gossip about which Cardinal did that, and what the Pope did wrong this time, or the scandals happening in the Church... we already know. It's not news! This organization is really messed up sometimes! But the errors of men don't mean we need God less. No one argues that the Church instructs, approves of, and endorses these awful behaviors. They obviously condemn it. And to air the dirty laundry out in public all the time would only dishearten those weaker in faith. Much in the same way parents don't let their young children know of all their struggles.

So, Church Politicking - please don't. It does not help you, or me, or anyone. Pray for the Church. Attend the Sacraments. Live well. That's the best we can do.

by Adam Thiede
