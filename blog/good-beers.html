Good Beers

[link—standalone]

Here's some good beer's I'd recommend, and the time of year in which they should be consumed. I don't like drinking dark beer in the spring or summer time, since to me, it feels like something you should have with a stew by the fire in winter. Just like hot coffee isn't great on a summer afternoon (cold brew for that!) and a milkshake might not be a great time on a cold day, different flavors of beer should only be quaffed when it's their perfect time of year. Of course, this is all my opinion, and you may have different taste from me. This list will be updated as I try new things.

  • Guinness - not a great beer, but the Best Beer. Drinkable in any season, but preferable fall/winter. Nice and smooth, a classic dark beer. Not very complex.
  • Sierra Nevada Hazy Little Thing - a fantastic go-to spring or summer beer, as is any hazy IPA. Sweet and fruity, simple, and just all around lovely.
  • Backwoods Pecan Pie Porter - Possibly my favorite "weird flavors" beer. Very nice flavor for before/after a cold night walk. Great in October..
  • Blue Moon - surprisingly I just had one of these a few weeks ago. I don't often go for non "craft" beers, but I had one after a run in the sun, and it was fantastic.
  • Deschutes Hand Up - Feels like a mix between an IPA and something a little more full-bodied and flavorful, like and ale. I've only had a few but something I'm warming to. 6/10, only because it's got a higher ABV than it has any right to. Good all year.
  • Left Hand Milk Stout - Nitro or GTFO! This is like a Guinness++. Nothing worse and everything's better. I restrict stouts to winter but with this one it's hard.
  • Arrogant Bastard - You probably wouldn't like it anyways, but it's worth a shot. It's a strong, tall boy. Fall.
  • Rogue Voodoo Doughnut - Had this one years ago, and I still remember it fondly. It was weird (of course it was) and I like weird, but it was a little too much for me. Probably don't drink it during a Kansas summer. That's likely what ruined it.
  • Riverbend Life's a Peach And Then You Die - it's a Peach Milkshake IPA. It's thick, cloudy, sweet, but not overly cider-y, still very beer-y. Delicous. Spring & summer. Possibly the one on this list I'm most fond of, mostly because my wife likes them too. Typically she likes ciders but this one's a great middle ground.

by Adam Thiede
