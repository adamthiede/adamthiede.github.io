My Dealine for Signal

TL;DR: December 2022

I've probably talked to you before about my desire to move back to fully open standards for my communication. Hardly a controversial stance. But I've not put a hard line on it. Now I am. At the end of the year 2022, I'll delete my Signal account and stop using it entirely. Moving forward I will be using encrypted email or XMPP or matrix. Pick one - they're all pretty easy.

"Why?":

  • If I don't, nobody will ever stop using it to communicate with me, and I have to force the transition.
  • I have to package my own Signal client for aarch64 Linux. They don't do it. I'm tired of the churn.
  • Choosing Signal over other proprietary garbage messengers is choosing one master over another. It's just choosing to be subject to the whims of a maybe less bad actor. I'd rather be my own master.
  • Open Standards are a more flexible choice and will likely be around longer. Hopefully this is the last hard-cut.
  • Read Protocols, not Platforms and Moving Back to XMPP.

Of course, the "drawbacks" are that you get to choose your own provider and client. I'll even host you if you want me to, but there are probably better servers out there.

If you have questions, talk to me using email or XMPP, or, for now, Signal.
