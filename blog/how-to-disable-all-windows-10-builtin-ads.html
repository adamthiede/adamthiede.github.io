This article caught my attention recently.

These always make me laugh. This is valid until a) they add more ad locations, b) they don't turn the settings back on automatically, c) they don't cripple the experience in other ways, require more interaction. It's always a race against how horribly Microsoft can cripple Windows, and if you can keep up with it via changing settings every week, fighting against dark patterns, or registry hacks.

Here's my 1 step list to get rid of ads in Windows:

1. Install GNU/Linux.

At this point nobody that's not an enterprise really needs Windows. GNU/Linux is very good and will probably work better for all but a small minority of computer users.

The common excuses people give are

  1. I need X specific program
  2. I play video games
  3. I don't want to learn something new/put in any effort/use my brain at all

You can use all but the most niche software on GNU/Linux, or there's an appropriate alternative for it. (Yes, that does mean use GIMP instead of Photoshop. If it has problems, you can fix them, or report them to the developers. That's how Open Source Software works.)

Most video games are playable via Steam+Proton, that is, you install Steam, check one box in Steam settings, and install your games. If they aren't, then play them on a console, or don't play them. You don't need to play video games. There also exist a bunch of open source games you can play. Might as well try out Xonotic, 0AD, Minetest, or even Roguelikes (nethack, crawl, rogue).

And finally if you are so opposed to freedom and just really want to keep the shackles on, I can't help you. Everything in life requires learning, and if you won't take the time to learn something incredibly valuable, go jump in a lake.
