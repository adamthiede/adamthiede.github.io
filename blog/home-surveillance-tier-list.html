Home Surveillance Tier List

[link—standalone]

So you want home security cameras? We'll start off with the easy/flaming garbage/entry level, then go all the way to Ultimate Ascendent Gamer God-Tier.

Easy/Flaming Garbage:

  • Nest/Ring/Arlo - basically self-surveillance. THEY should be paying YOU for this. Don't buy. The police can access this stuff whenever they want, and even if you like the police, this is bad. Also you pay a monthly fee.
  • Hikvision - a company sponsored and staffed by the Chinese government. Even though they're technically and physically good, it's like allowing the Chinese government into your home, email, banking, and private thoughs. There's a reason they cost 1/3 the price of something good.

"I did slightly more research than clicking on an ad" tier:

  • Wyze cameras - they're ok. Still pretty creepy spyware, but at least it's not going directly to big tech. The low cost and free monitoring makes it at least worth considering.

1337 Hackerman tier:

  • Raspberry Pi + Open Source solutions - Shinobi, ZoneMinder, Blue Iris, etc. are all pretty fun little hacks to run on a low cost credit card sized computer. These solutions cost next to nothing, and are highly modular. Add whatever cameras you like! You can even combine them with high tier stuff like Axis cameras, or low tier stuff like Wyze cameras (with the RTSP firmware).

"I have more money than sense" Tier:

  • Ubiquiti UniFi cameras - good stuff. They don't operate with other systems, you have to buy all their stuff, they send loads of telementry you can't turn off, but this is very high quality gear that will last for decades. Real businesses deploy this stuff. Ubiquiti is good. If you like Apple products and don't like learning, buy these.

Ultimate Ascendent Super-Saiyan God Tier:

  • Build your own NVR setup with A NAS/home server and RTSP/ONVIF cameras - you care about this stuff. It matters to you. You have 8 disks in RAID, offsite backups, a UPS, a PoE Switch, and 17 Axis cameras bolted to your house. You run OpenBSD on your desktop.

This list is a joke.
