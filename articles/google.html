Alternatives to Google Products

For every Google product (that they've not killed yet) there is a better alternative. From Search to Chromebooks, here's your list of links! Some of these cost money! If you're not paying and it's not clear how it's being funded, you're the product. Some of them are Free and Open Source Software maintained by volunteers, and a labor of love. "You're the product" doesn't apply here.

Search

This is the easiest one. duckduckgo, Brave search, or searx (self-hostable). There is no reason to be using Google Search. They show you what they want you to see for their benefit, not for yours.

Gmail

Another easy one! mailbox.org, posteo, startmail, fidei.email, and migadu. You can also self-host email of course. Most of these come with Calendar and Contacts integrated too. That's two more off the list. Yes, you can send calendar appointments to other people still on gmail or hotmail or business email or whatever. Migrating email is hard. Block out a few hours to do it. You'll want to do it in one day, or else you'll never do it.

Chrome

Chrome is the worst browser. Others offer more user-centric features by default, are faster, and actually don't spy on you. What a concept. Use Firefox or Brave. Migration is like 3 clicks. You have no excuse!

Drive/Docs/Photos

Nextcloud is software you put on a server. If that's not your thing, there are hosted instances. Cryptpad is a bit simpler, can also be run on your server, or you could sign up at a hosted instance. Proton Drive also exists but it has the same centralized problem. It's only a little better than Google. Photos are just files; store them with your files. For a nice interface, check out PhotoPrism.
If you really want to own your digital data, you can buy a server and run either TrueNas, Umbrel, YunoHost, FreedomBox, OpenMediaVault, on your hardware in your home.

Android

Android is difficult. Most of the alternatives are just security-focused Android forks. Obviously this is still Android, but much like Chromium-based browsers you don't have too much choices outside of it. I have plenty of recommendations in this article about smartphones. I recommend postmarketOS which is real Linux for phones. Droidian is Linux on the Android kernels and drivers, which is less secure, but also an option.

ChromeOS

Unlike regular computuers, Chromebooks and Macs make it hard to replace the OS. If you can manage the technical piece your Chormebook will behave like a normal computer save for the weird keyboard. Otherwise if you have a regular computer, or can get one, use Linux Mint for simplicity, familiarity, and stability, or Fedora for up to security, up to date software, and good developer experience.

Messaging

Hahhahahaahahha Google's messaging stragtegy is such a bust that you can pick anything else and it will be better. XMPP is a gold standard. Basically email, but it's chat instead. Sign up for a provider, then pick a client program. Sign up here and use Conversations on Android (or the Android forks mentioned above) and gajim or dino on Linux.
Matrix is an up-and-comer but it's heavier and more complicated than XMPP.
Signal is easy but it's centralized, so retains the "central authority" problem like Google. Plus it's overly complicated, has a rough history, and is becoming social media.
