Raspberry Pis and SD Cards

[link—standalone]

For proabably the fourth time in five years, my Raspberry Pi computer has destroyed another microSD card.

This happens so infrequently (about every 18 months) that I'm willing to mostly overlook it, but the problem is it always surprises me. I have a high-minded idea that every month I'm going to take my SD card out on a weekend, image it, and keep a few backups, but it ends up being about once every six months, and usually the card dies at the end of those months. Frustrating!

So, since I have a NAS, I've been slowly moving the Pi's functionality into Docker containers on the NAS. The storage there is slow but reliable, and it'll send me an email if a disk fails. Plus, these drives will likely have a much longer lifespan than a microSD card, given they're NAS-grade hard drives. Most of what I did on the Pi was not services that required a lot of horsepower anyway, and since the NAS as a celeron and 6 GB of RAM, it's able to run Pi-Hole, Syncthing, and a few others much faster than the Pi itself did.

It makes me sad though, because I love the Pi family, and I wish there was a better way to have it not kill storage so frequently. I've looked into using a flash drive or USB hard drive as external storage, but that just seems like kicking dead whales down the beach. In the end, maybe the Pi's better off doing simple stuff (like the one running the "can you wake up your parents yet" light in my daughter's room) rather than trying to make it a decent server. The Pi's served me faithfully for years, but now that I have a real server, it just doesn't compare.

by Adam Thiede
