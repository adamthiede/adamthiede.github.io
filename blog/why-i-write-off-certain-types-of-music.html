Why I Write Off Certain Types Of Music

[link—standalone]

I figured out why I don't like country or most rap.

A brief sample of what I do listen to: classical music, jazz, good rock & roll, soundtracks, alternative rock from the 90's and 2000s (and yes, that "lo-fi" ambient music that's trendy these days; I happen to think it's good pleasant background music to work to). Feel free to write off my opinions now before you go any further!

I like this music because it stands alone. It's just music, and it sounds good, or it doesn't. It doesn't say "look at how cool I the singer am" and it doesn't require you to buy into the validity or primacy of a specific lifestyle. Rap is frequently about "look at me and how cool and rich I am, and let me describe for you my sexual prowess and/or how many people I've killed". Country's basically the same, but instead of flashy gold necklaces, fancy cars, and expensive booze it's a different set of objects (jeans and cowboy hats, tractors and old trucks, cheap beer), the descriptions of sex are much more subtle, and the violence is non-lethal.

They're both all about a specific lifestyle and how it's cool, instead of, you know, the music. It's an advertisement, basically.

If you haven't figured it out yet, I'm a fan of meritocracy. Anyway, that's it. Happy Saturday.

by Adam Thiede
