Breaking Out

[link—standalone]

Simple steps, to follow in order, to break free of the shackles of social media:

  • Make your last post on your account. Write something like "Social media is a not a good way to spend time, and I've had enough. My phone number is X, my email address is Y, and you can contact me through those if you want to talk. I encourage you all to join me, and we can create a group email list or group chat on Signal."
  • Change the passwords to something really long and random, like "p-mSM^n19CFCXD#JLpGzsY%gcU". Optionally, have a trusted friend change the passwords for you, so you can't log in.
  • Delete the apps, if you have them on your phone. Selectively delete them from your browser history or your bookmarks. Log out of all active sessions.
  • Wait a month or three. See how you feel.
  • During this time, read a book, write a journal, or make a website. Contemplate how you feel.
  • Write down a list of things you might need to log back into your account for - if you've signed up for any services with the "Sign In with Facebook" button, you'll need to reconfigure those.
  • Log back into the accounts and delete them after an appropriate amount of time has passed.
  • Remember - if a person will only talk to you on social media, and not via personal messages, email, phone calls, or the real world, they're not your friend, and you're better off not wasting time with them.
I sincerely hope this helps you free your mind and love yourself.

by Adam Thiede
