Responsibility, Availability, and Free Software

[link—standalone]

I'm conflicted, and want to ask for advice.

But first, two short anecdotes.

  • When I was in college, I was available and reachable to my roommate. He woke me up late at night. His girlfriend's sister was sick and needed a ride to the hospital. I had a car. Since I was available, right there in his home with him, he asked me, and I gave him my keys. This enabled him to do something good for his girlfriend (now wife), and I was helpful and useful because I was available.
  • Shortly after college, I got a text message late in the evening from a friend. Because I was reachable, and available, I was able to respond to her need quickly and with love and attention. Now we're married.
Because I was reachable and available to my friends, I was able to affect positive change in my life, or help a friend out. I think that it is important to be reachable and available to one's friends.

Some definitions are required for me to be properly understood.
available: being immediately and easily able to freely give of one's time. Example: "Mary has a flexible job and takes every break she can to go out with her friends. She is always available."
reachable: being able to receive and respond to requests in a timely fashion. Example: "Colin answers his phone on the first ring, and responds to emails within the hour. He's very reachable."

I have a full-time job and a family. I have some hobbies and projects that I enjoy. I'm not always available but I should not be difficult to reach. I stay at home most of the time, have a good Internet connection, and have a lot of computers that are frequently on. It shouldn't be hard to get open a communication channel. There are a lot of ways to contact me.

Should I have a preference on how people should contact me with computers?
Should I, as I have in the past, enforce that preference?

But I worry about this a lot. I worry that my extreme preference for Free Software is (funnily enough) excluding people from being able to interact with me. Now, that doesn't make a lot of sense to me, because if I insist on Free Software, no one should be prohibited from contacting me. The only barrier is convenience. If the person on the other end for whatever reason refuses Freedom, I guess there's nothing I can do about it.

However, "don't make perfect the enemy of good" - if something is good enough, I should let it exist and work. I shouldn't put a bunch of silly overly-technical flaming hoops up for people to jump through, just to call me on the phone. It seems to confuse people that I actually do care about how they contact me. I just want it to be safe and reliable - unfortunately that's kind of a hard problem to solve these days.

All this to say, I want to use the weird computer stuff I want to use, because I'm a guy who likes weird computers. However, I use communication solutions that are, by their very nature, available to anyone and everyone, regardless of what they use. I don't put up barriers. I don't force people to do unethical things. It might be slightly more difficult than laying on the couch, eating chips, and channel surfing, but come on, you're better than that anyway.

Anyway. That's about it. I want to be reachable, because I am available. I want to be friendly and helpful and reliable to my friends and family. I believe that I am, and that enforcing my communication channels to be good things, but not necessarily easy, is kind of what I'm here for. Why even use a computer to talk to me if you're not interested in that sort of thing?

by Adam Thiede
