COVID, Power, and Taking a Stand

Please don't, but you can skip to the good part if you're impatient, or already believe and trust me.

I love rules.

I love understanding rules, and following them. After all, living in a civilized society, we all agree to abide by a set of rules, and that's what keeps society civil, sane, and safe. Growing up in Oregon, aerial fireworks were theoretically illegal, but every July 4th, the neighborhood would agree to send some families across the river into Washington, to obtain the "illegals". (Yes, that's what we called them.) I was always distraught about this and eventually stopped participating in the fireworks mess altogether as a pre-teen, as a protest against this flagrant rule-breaking.

Rules are what keep our society safe. But they only work when the rules are reasonable and useful. Sometimes, someone refuses to leave a diner or the front of a bus, or uses the wrong drinking fountain on purpose, and breaks the rules. We agree that these were bad rules, stop enforcing them, and eventually take them out of the rule set. Critically, it must happen in this order. The rules are broken first, and then they are removed second. It takes a spark of inspiration before we all slowly, eventually come to the right conclusions. Often those in power seek to retain their power through the enforcement of dangerous or harmful rules. (See Gerrymandering, if you ever had any doubt) So it does us good to question rules, try and see the reasons behind them, and figure out if they're good for us in the long-term, or if they're mainly built to keep people in power, and trick the majority of the population into doing something harmful.

On Resisting For The Wrong Reasons

A lot of the more tech-savvy individuals these days seem to be a little hesitant on developing Artificial Intelligence, how far we should take it, and how fast we should get there. Hesitancy in the roll-out of a potentially world-altering technology is a good thing. Being cautious, looking before you leap, and trying to be prepared before diving headfirst into the unknowable future is a good thing.

Now that doesn't mean we should fight every rule, vote down every tax increase, throw eggs at politicians' cars, and drive on the wrong side of the road because the people in power are all bad. That's not the case. But we should be hesitant to be on their "side", because they are not on ours. We should be hesitant of accepting what they tell us without verification from those we do trust. Note that this mostly goes for people in rather powerful positions; the governor of your rural town is probably not mad with power. Take what I say with a grain of salt too - I'm rather imperfect myself.

So don't think of this piece as a resistance against authority because "F the police" or "Orange Man Bad" or "Let's Go Brandon" reasons. That's not it. It's just an application of healthy skepticism against what is being force-fed through every avenue these days. Seems like people used to encourage that a lot more often.

Here's The Good Part

I don't think the currently active coronavirus was developed on purpose and released on purpose. That's a little too far-fetched for me. It likely developed due to inhumane living conditions and a lack of cleanliness as most of us are aware. They've got it rough over there in China. But many politicians are known to espouse the "never let a good crisis go to waste" motto, and so this one's obviously being milked for all it's worth. Just like war, there are always groups that can stand to make a whole heckload of profit when things like this happen. Making folks afraid, making them distrust each other, and giving them an easy thing to buy or support to help quell that fear, is the oldest marketing trick in the book. Think of the children. You wouldn't want someone to break into your house, buy this alarm system/gun. The terrorists are coming, vote for me to bring the fight to them. You're being tracked, buy my VPN. (This is a good time to tell you I earn no money off of you visiting this site. I have no ads, no tracking, collect no information of any kind, and nobody pays me to write this. I hardly check logs on the webserver. It is costing me money to make this site available.)

So why isn't that classical healthy dose of skeptisism being applied to the "solutions" that the government has proposed for COVID-19? Do people really just trust the US Government and Big Pharma now? Do we not remember the infinite war on terror, the constant support/ousting of dictators in other countries, and oh yeah, the NSA and Edward Snowden? Three years ago you could hardly hear someone say "Big Pharma" without spitting! They profit from our misery, keep us dependent on them with infinite booster shots and stimulus checks, keep us hating our neighbors, and are creating a new class of untouchables in our society.
