People Want Crutches

[link—standalone]

I'm not one for short term solutions (see here). Amongst my family I'm sometimes treated as the "go-to guy" for anything involving a plug. Almost without fail, I try a long-term fix every single time someone asks me for a solution. I try to "teach a man to fish", to borrow a turn of phrase.

Overwhelmingly, this does not work.

People think I'm talking in too complicated of terms, they think I'm ridiculing them, and usually throw their hands up and either abandon their quest, or just ask me to do it for them. The latter takes some humility, which I can respect, but usually I refuse these requests. I want everyone to be smart about computers. I want more people to talk with about a very important thing in my life. I would love it if I could install Gentoo with my mom. That would be awesome. But I would rather have someone abandon computers entirely, do paper billing, use a rotary phone, and live in a cabin in the woods, than get used to using computer-smart people as a crutch.

It's your stuff. Your PC, your Personal Computer is about you. I want you to have the freedom and the ownership. I want you to have the responsibility, but also the power. Computers are unlike anything else. They're highly integrated and personalized by their owner.

I understand the feeling of desperation when things aren't working out. I feel the same way when someone asks me to do something for them that I've tried to teach. I just want people to get better, not rely on me. But usually, I'm disappointed to find that people want crutches.

by Adam Thiede
