This Week's Adventures

[link—standalone]

On December 19 I traveled to California to visit with my brother and parents. The plan was to fly back home on the 23rd, to be with my own family for Christmas. However, my airline had other ideas . Southwest, and others, cancelled record amounts of flights, and after about six hours in the airport, I had to head back to where my parents and brother were staying, resigned to wait it out. Although a disappointing situation, I was far better off than most. Many were stuck in the airport for weeks, but since I had family close by, who had intened to stay there until the 26th, I was fortunate. Flights for the 26th were booked and I had Christmas with one part of my family.

On the 26th my parents drove back from California to their home. I rode with them to hitch a ride to a different airport where I had booked my latest flight home. On the way, I was notified my flight was cancelled, so I didn't even stop and just continued along driving with my parents the rest of the way to their house. Again, I am fortunate I had places to go throughout this whole ordeal. Praise God.

Along the way I bought a Worst Buy Special (a decidedly mediocre $650 laptop) so I could work from my parents' house. I did not decide to bring any computers on this trip, to focus on time with my family, and that was a mistake! I could've just taken the week off, but many others already were under the premise I would be there, so I felt like I needed to make an effort. The laptop came with Windows 11. I attempted to immediately replace it with 10, but that didn't work. Once it's tricked into using a local account and all the preinstalled garbage (TikTok and Instagram and Netflix and Disney+, yes truly) is removed, Windows isn't too terrible. I know now I'd rather use it than macOS, despite the latter's Unix-like underpinnings. Windows 11's terminal, chocolatey, and power toys made the experience less painful.

Eventually I made it back home with this weird laptop in tow. I didn't attempt Linux on the road becuase it was an unknown, and sound didn't work on a Fedora live USB, so I wasn't going to chance anything. To run Linux on this laptop there are three four main caveats:

  1. Audio from the speakers does not work by default. Still looking. They use the SOF driver apparently so maybe there's hope!
  2. The laptop will not resume from suspend unless a custom kernel patch is applied and the kernel is rebuilt. I'm running this now. Works well but I wish it was configurable instead of requiring a recompile.
  3. The webcam does not work (maybe related).
  4. The touchscreen doesn't work. I don't really care about this.
So that's what I'm working with. It's quite fast, the screen is nice, it's a good machine overall. Checks most of my boxes. I did not at all need another laptop but I'm not displeased with the form and function of it. The BIOS sucks and I'll likely need a FreeDOS stick or something to update it, since I think the whole "update BIOS from the BIOS" thing is reserved for the "business" lines for some reason. I hate proprietary firmware. Especially since it's the firmware's brokenness that necessitates the kernel patch.

Because some flights got cancelled I ended up with a new laptop (that I may still attempt to return) and had to recompile Linux. The world's weird sometimes. Merry Christmas and Happy New Year.

by Adam Thiede
