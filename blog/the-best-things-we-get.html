The Best Things We Get

[link—standalone]

When our retired Italian neighbor up the street graces our dinner table with his homemade spaghetti, it's basically the only thing we eat until it's gone. We eat cold spaghetti for breakfast. I'm racing my wife to the fridge at noon to see who can get the first crack at the foil-clad ceramic treasure chest.

Occasionally I'm reminded that the best things we get aren't things we can buy, or things that we can do for ourselves. The best days of the past six months I remember are times when my family was together, when I was out in nature with my best friends, and when I'm sharing something I know with others. The times that it really hits me are usually food-based - taste is a dramatic sense to me, maybe because my diet is so boring most of the time.

The best things we get come from when others do things with and for us. For me the big part is the sense of gratitutde and love that I get from knowing that someone though of me and my family, and went out of their way to do something kind for us, like inviting us to stay for a weekend, or bringing us a meal, or giving us a specific thing that we didn't even know we needed.

Friends are great, and especially this year it's been great to have interaction with friends. They light up our lives in ways we can't ourselves. Whoever you are reading this, you're probably a friend. Thanks for being in my life!

by Adam Thiede
