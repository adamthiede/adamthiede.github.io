If you want to give me a gift, please do so according to the following guidelines. Items are listed in order of preference. I am quite particular about goods that I use.


  • Get me something from one of my public wishlists. First: Adafruit. Microcontrollers and other electronics. Second: Amazon. General stuff.
  • I like books, especially technical books. I prefer physical books, but DRM-free ePub files are also a lovely gift.
  • One can never own too many kettlebells. (local shop, national shop) These are good gifts for me.
  • Bandcamp is the place I buy music. They offer gift cards that can be exchanged for DRM-free, lossless audio files.
  • Coffee beans, tea, and beer are also wonderful gifts. I'll always be thankful for some strange craft stout.
  • I have enough clothing, am particular about how clothing fits, and my body is not the size or shape you think it is. I will deal with this myself. Steph and Tom, you are the exceptions to this rule.
  • I like computers and "computer stuff", but again, am very particular. I will do it myself.
  • A notable exception is Thinkpads, MacBooks, or desktop systems at least 10 years old - if you have an old computer that is physically clean (I will not accept something caked in gunk) I will be overjoyed to play with it.
  • These are the places I accept money. If you direct me how to spend it (in abstract) I will abide by the suggestion, i.e. "Use it for coffee" or "Buy your wife some art supplies".
  • Lastly, please do not feel pressured to get me gifts on holidays or birthdays. I am personally terrible at gift-giving and will probably not reciprocate. Let's instead have a meaningful conversation on my back porch over a beverage - I guarantee the memory of that will last longer than whatever you give me.
