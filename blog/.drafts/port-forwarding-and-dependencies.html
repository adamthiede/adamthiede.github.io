Time for a tale of mystery and horror. Every so often my parents call me about computer/network/services problems, and I always solve them free of charge since because they dealt with me for 18+ years, and also I love them and stuff.

My dad has this thing in his house that's an internet-connected box that controls physical things in his house (so, already a bad idea; we've all seen that episode of Mr. Robot). It's got a bunch of outputs - CEC, IR, (and RS232 and Ethernet, neither used at this point) that control the entertainment center stuff and switch between inputs. He and my mom can control it on the iPad (which is what he likes, 🙄 ). There's also a big physical remote with BlackBerry-style wheel and back button on it, which is AWESOME. Anyhow, the computer box portion of it broke.

So this internet connected mystery computer that has unlimited access to his home network (even though I gave him a guest network with isolation!) needs to get an update from the manufacturer to re-enable iPad control because that was the part that broke. So he calls the technicians and they say they can do it via teamviewer, since it's corona time, and nobody wants visitors. He calls me today, frantically asking for the admin password for his router (which I mysteriously have, and he even more mysteriously does not) so he can give it to a technician. I obviously tell him no.

But then he explains that the technician is going to remote into his mac, change his router settings to set up two port forwards into this mystery box, and then he'll update it from outside.

Anyway, my dad conferences me in with this guy, and since I can't drop everything and offer my dad a better solution in the next 20 minutes, I guess this thing has to happen. I insist the technician remove the port forwards after he is done with them. He agrees. While we're on the call, he causally mentions that most of the time, they don't have to have port forwards, because they usually put VPN servers in these boxes. "One button, and then bang, I'm in your home network!" OH MY GOD. This box doesn't need to be Internet connected or even network connected, but yet, here we are.

Anyway, moral of the story - given the least charitable explanation, how comfortable are you with the stuff in your home? If you're putting something like this in your home, it should make you less dependent overall. I get universal remotes - less dependent on remembering button layout, less frustration overall, more time for relaxation and enjoyment. But your universal remote should never just randomly break for months, then require 2 hours of your time and a technician with port forwards. Strive for less dependence on technology. That's why when I write, I put it in .txt files, permanently readable by any type of software, old or new. I store data in transparent, open filesystems, in common formats. I don't have anything on my network that I can't cobble together a solution for myself. Nothing controls my computing lifestyle, in a way.

When you're going to compromise your safety, make sure it's for something really really good. This ain't it, chief.
