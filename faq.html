About Me / FAQ


Hi there, welcome to my website. Glad you could stop by. Let me tell you who I am, what I do, and why this site exists.


There's a lot that goes on in my day, but work takes up the biggest slice. My job goes by many titles - Systems Engineer, Systems Administrator, DevOps Engineer, Systems Analyst, IT Guy, "Computer Guy" (my personal favorite). I know loads about computers, in both a specific and general sense, and I am paid to make them work well. I can do anything from setting up a laptop with a printer all the way up to configuring network infrastructure. You should care because I can help you do anything you want with computers!


This site is pretty barebones - it's not fancy or 'modern' and doesn't use trendy web technologies, but it works better than most of the sites that are. It's just a few HTML files on a tiny web server. No trendy frameworks, no JavaScript, no weird dependencies, and I wrote it all by hand. Simplicity is beautiful, and I'm saving you data and battery life.


This site is both a portfolio and blog. The blog is focused on the values of freedom, independence, and longevity, and how computers help or hurt those goals. My belief system puts me somewhere between 100% Free Software Purist and Reluctant Pragmantist - I will recommend Free Software all of the time, and relent to proprietary software only when it is the only option. Software Freedom is one of the most important issues of our time - since computers are everything and everywhere, we must ensure the computers we use respect individual freedom and privacy. I value simplicity, interoperability, stability, security, and reproducibility, and only Free Software will give you that - anything else is a mystery.
