> some totally reasonable person you mail, doing totally reasonable things, will invariably CC the quoted plaintext of your encrypted message to someone else

Ouch, too real for me.

I use pass, and it's a wrapper for PGP that encrypts passwords. Despite that (and PGP email at work) I don't really use PGP at all these days. It's not a great solution for any problem.

With the rapid churn of various chat clients (WhatsApp was good until it wasn't, same with Skype,  AIM, etc) it's hard to get something to stick, and I attempted to settle on XMPP+OMEMO (or OTR). Not a single person I know still uses XMPP, and it's easier to just tell people "Download Signal". However, that requires a phone number, and is impossible to set up on a desktop your phone's off. There has to be a better solution, and despite email+PGP's flaws, it's the only system I've seen that's stood the test of time.
