Computer hardware is the "root of trust". If your hardware is compromised, nothing else you do matters.

Buy your hardware with wide support for many types of software in mind. Buy hardware that is common, widely understood, and less than 4 years old. A good example is Lenovo Thinkpads and Dell Latitudes. They are built for businesses to use, so they're tough, last a long time, and business cast-offs can be had for cheap. It's easy to find parts and repair these devices. Repairability is important.

Don't buy computers that are hard to work on and manufacturer-controlled, like (post-2015) Apple computers. Smartphones and other gadgets like them are difficult to recommend, because they are also difficult to trust and depend on the manufacturer to keep them safe. The Google Pixel line tend to be modification friendly, and they provide an easy way to restore the device should things go wrong. If you must use a smartphone, get one of these, and install an aftermarket operating system. (This is covered in the Software section)

System76 and Think Penguin are good companies to buy ready-to-go computers from. No Big Tech involved - they ship set up to respect your freedom and privacy.

When buying new devices, buy from big-box stores in cash, in person. For used devices, buy in person on Craigslist and inspect the device beforehand, or buy from Swappa, who has a good return policy.
